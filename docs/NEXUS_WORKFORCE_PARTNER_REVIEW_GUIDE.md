# Nexus Workforce Partner Review Guide

## Jobs And Workforce Overview

Nexus helps users explore job readiness, role pathways, skills gaps, and preparation steps. It is intended to support workers, youth, rural learners, and training partners with plain-language guidance before any real application, employer contact, or credential workflow.

Nexus does not submit applications, contact employers, guarantee jobs, issue credentials, or replace partner review.

## Training And Literacy Support Overview

Nexus can help users review training steps, literacy-friendly explanations, captions, language support, and learning pathways. This is source-backed guidance and preparation only.

Training support does not certify completion, grant credentials, or submit records unless a later approved partner workflow is configured.

## Youth And Rural Workforce Pathway Review

Review whether Nexus supports:

- first-job readiness;
- agriculture-to-workforce pathways;
- rural service roles;
- technical training;
- digital literacy;
- language and literacy support;
- practical next steps for local programs;
- respectful guidance for users with limited formal experience.

## Skills Checklist Review

Workforce partners should review whether skills checklists are:

- understandable;
- locally relevant;
- aligned with real training programs;
- realistic for beginner users;
- clear about missing prerequisites;
- careful not to claim employment, certification, or placement.

## Questions For Workforce And Training Partners

1. Are job pathways understandable for rural and youth users?
2. Does Nexus avoid promising employment or credential outcomes?
3. Are skills checklists practical and complete enough?
4. Does the workflow support literacy and multilingual needs?
5. What local program information should be source-backed before pilot use?
6. What partner review is needed before any credential or application workflow?
7. Are there phrases that could be misunderstood as application submission?
8. Which workforce lanes are most suitable for a first controlled pilot?
