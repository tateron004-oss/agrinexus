# Nexus Provider Review Package

## Purpose Of Provider Review

This package gives real-world reviewers a structured way to evaluate AgriNexus / Nexus before any live regulated or real-world execution is enabled. The review goal is to test whether Nexus is useful, clear, safe, locally relevant, and ready for controlled pilot users.

Provider review is not a live service launch. Reviewers are asked to inspect preparation, organization, source-backed guidance, and provider-review workflows.

## What AgriNexus / Nexus Is

AgriNexus is the broader platform for agriculture, workforce, marketplace preparation, community support, health access preparation, maps, source trust, and offline support.

Nexus is the assistant layer that helps users organize questions, prepare next steps, review source-backed guidance, and understand what requires human provider review or future approved integrations.

## What Providers Are Being Asked To Evaluate

Reviewers should evaluate:

- whether the language is understandable for rural, workforce, agriculture, and health-access users;
- whether preparation outputs are useful before meeting a real provider or partner;
- whether safety boundaries are clear;
- whether Nexus asks for the right missing information;
- whether source-backed guidance is framed correctly;
- whether the app avoids overpromising live actions;
- whether the provider-review posture is appropriate for pilot users.

## What Nexus Can Safely Do

Nexus may:

- prepare and organize user questions;
- summarize non-sensitive synthetic test scenarios;
- generate review-only provider report drafts;
- explain health access preparation steps;
- organize chronic care questions for later provider review;
- provide source-backed agriculture and workforce education;
- prepare marketplace or communications notes without sending them;
- show review-only dashboard modes and safety labels;
- identify when provider, partner, or expert review is required.

## What Nexus Cannot Do

Nexus must not:

- diagnose, treat, prescribe, or replace clinical judgment;
- handle emergencies or dispatch emergency services;
- book appointments or create live telehealth sessions;
- contact providers, buyers, sellers, employers, service organizations, or care teams;
- send calls, SMS, WhatsApp, Telegram, email, or other messages;
- process payments, purchases, refunds, checkout, or money movement;
- share location, request browser geolocation, or start navigation handoff;
- activate camera, capture images, or perform image-based diagnosis;
- create backend writes that represent real pending actions;
- perform autonomous real-world execution.

## Provider Categories

- Physicians and care teams: health access preparation, chronic care preparation, and provider report builder review.
- Agriculture experts and extension officers: crop issue guidance, source-backed agriculture support, and local expert confirmation language.
- Workforce and training partners: job readiness, training pathways, literacy support, skills checklists, and rural/youth workforce fit.
- Marketplace and community partners: AgriTrade inquiry preparation, community service preparation, and no-execution marketplace boundaries.
- Service organizations: transportation-to-care preparation, mobile clinic access preparation, offline support, and source trust review.

## Review Process

1. Review this package and the relevant role-specific concept note or guide.
2. Use synthetic, sample, or explicitly approved test data only.
3. Start with the Standard User dashboard.
4. Review the relevant preparation modes and safety labels.
5. Run the recommended synthetic prompts or scenarios.
6. Score the experience with the provider feedback rubric.
7. Note any wording that could be misunderstood as diagnosis, prescribing, booking, contact, payment, dispatch, location sharing, or execution.
8. Recommend whether the workflow is ready for controlled pilot users, needs revision, or should remain internal.

## Recommended Demo Flow

Use the normal Standard User flow:

1. Open the app.
2. Start as User.
3. Review the Nexus command center.
4. Select a mode relevant to the reviewer:
   - Health Access Preparation
   - Chronic Care Preparation
   - Provider Report Builder
   - Agriculture Support
   - Crop Issue Guidance
   - Jobs & Workforce
   - Training & Literacy
   - Marketplace / AgriTrade
   - Community Services
   - Source Trust / Citation Support
5. Confirm the selected mode uses labels such as Preparation Only, Review Only, Provider Review Required, Source-Backed Guidance, Preview, or Coming Soon.
6. Confirm no real-world action is executed from the dashboard.

## Feedback Collection Process

Collect feedback in three parts:

- Scored rubric: usefulness, clarity, safety, accuracy, local relevance, workflow fit, misunderstanding risk, and pilot readiness.
- Open notes: what worked, what confused the reviewer, what was missing, and what should be reworded.
- Safety flags: any language that appears to diagnose, prescribe, book, contact, pay, share location, dispatch, or execute.

## Safety And Privacy Reminders

- Use synthetic or approved test data only.
- Do not enter real patient records, protected health information, payment information, credentials, precise live location, or emergency information.
- Nexus preparation outputs are not medical advice, prescriptions, emergency support, live provider contact, or completed transactions.
- Any future live provider connector must require consent, permission, explicit approval, audit logging, provider availability, and compliance review before activation.
