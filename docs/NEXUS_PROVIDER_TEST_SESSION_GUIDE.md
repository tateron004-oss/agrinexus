# Nexus Provider Test Session Guide

## Session Purpose

Use this guide to run controlled review sessions with physicians, agriculture experts, workforce partners, marketplace/community partners, and service organizations. The session goal is to collect structured feedback without enabling real-world execution.

## What To Say Before The Session

Use this opening:

> Nexus is being reviewed as a preparation and guidance assistant. In this test round, it can help organize questions, prepare review-only notes, and show source-backed guidance. It does not diagnose, prescribe, contact providers, book appointments, send messages, process payments, share location, dispatch services, or complete transactions.

Also remind reviewers:

- use synthetic or approved test data only;
- do not enter real patient records, payment information, credentials, precise live location, or emergency information;
- report any wording that could be misunderstood as a completed action.

## What To Avoid Saying

Do not say:

- Nexus can diagnose or treat health conditions.
- Nexus can prescribe or refill medication.
- Nexus can contact providers, make calls, send messages, or book appointments.
- Nexus can process payments or complete marketplace transactions.
- Nexus can share live location, dispatch transportation, or handle emergencies.
- Nexus replaces provider, expert, partner, or clinical judgment.

## Physician Review Session

1. Start with Health Access Preparation.
2. Review Chronic Care Preparation.
3. Review Provider Report Builder.
4. Use synthetic health scenarios only.
5. Ask whether the language clearly avoids diagnosis, prescribing, treatment changes, appointment booking, provider contact, and emergency handling.
6. Ask whether provider-report drafts are useful and safe for human review.

Collect:

- clinical safety concerns;
- missing context questions;
- privacy concerns;
- emergency-language concerns;
- wording that could confuse patients;
- readiness for controlled pilot review.

## Agriculture Expert Review Session

1. Start with Agriculture Support.
2. Review Crop Issue Guidance.
3. Review Source Trust / Citation Support.
4. Use synthetic farmer scenarios only.
5. Ask whether Nexus gathers the right field context and frames local expert confirmation clearly.
6. Confirm Nexus does not diagnose crop disease, prescribe chemicals, activate camera, buy inputs, or dispatch services.

Collect:

- local source data needs;
- agronomy wording concerns;
- local expert confirmation gaps;
- crop issue questions to add;
- source trust and citation notes.

## Workforce Partner Review Session

1. Start with Jobs & Workforce.
2. Review Training & Literacy.
3. Use youth, rural, first-job, and training pathway examples.
4. Ask whether skills checklists are useful and realistic.
5. Confirm Nexus does not submit applications, contact employers, issue credentials, or promise placement.

Collect:

- pathway clarity feedback;
- local training program needs;
- literacy and language support notes;
- skills checklist improvements;
- pilot-user readiness.

## Marketplace And Community Review Session

1. Review Marketplace / AgriTrade.
2. Review Community Services.
3. Review Communications Preparation if relevant.
4. Confirm all outputs remain draft-only, review-only, and non-executing.
5. Confirm Nexus does not contact buyers, sellers, service providers, or organizations.

Collect:

- inquiry preparation usefulness;
- missing local service categories;
- transaction safety concerns;
- wording that could imply sending, payment, dispatch, or completed contact.

## Finding Classification

Classify findings as:

- Safety blocker: could imply diagnosis, prescribing, emergency handling, provider contact, payment, location sharing, camera, call, message, booking, transaction, or execution.
- Functional defect: app behavior does not match the expected Standard User flow.
- Wording issue: language is confusing but does not create immediate safety risk.
- Missing content: needs additional source data, local context, examples, or partner information.
- Pilot improvement: useful enhancement before broader controlled testing.

Safety blockers must be fixed before additional reviewer sessions.
